Checkbox & radio

Two selection controls that share one look. A checkbox selects any number of options; a radio selects exactly one. Both fill gold and pop their mark in on select.

When to use it

Use a checkbox when each option is independent and people may pick none, one, or many — or for a single opt-in like accepting terms. Use a radio group when the options are mutually exclusive and one must always be chosen. If the set has just two or three peer views, a segmented control may read better; for an instant on/off setting, use a switch.

  • Give every group a heading and every control a label people can click.
  • Radios in one group must share a name; pre-select a sensible default.
  • Keep labels parallel in phrasing and order them predictably.
  • Don’t use a single radio — a lone yes/no is a checkbox or a switch.

Anatomy

1 2 3
  • 1 Box — 22px control; rounded square for checkbox, circle for radio.
  • 2 Mark — a check glyph or a filled dot that scales in on select.
  • 3 Label — clickable text tied to the control.

Do & don’t

Do
Use radios for one-of-many, with a default already chosen.
Don’t
Don’t use checkboxes when only one answer can be true.
