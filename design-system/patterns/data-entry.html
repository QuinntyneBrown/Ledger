Data entry

Logging a weight is the one thing a person does in Ledger every single day, so it has to be the fastest thing in the app. The weigh-in sheet rises from the bottom, lands on a large stepper pre-filled with a sensible guess, and confirms with a single tap — three seconds, eyes barely open.

The weigh-in sheet

A bottom sheet — not a full page — keeps the person anchored to where they were. It carries a grip handle, the date, the stepper, and one primary action. Everything optional (backdating, quick-add) is one reach away but never in the path of the default case.

The numeric stepper

The centrepiece is a display-sized tabular numeral flanked by two round steppers. It opens pre-filled with the person's last reading so most days are a single tap — the number nudges ±0.1 kg per press, or opens a keypad on tap. Big targets, no keyboard, no hunting.

Quick-add

Below the stepper, a row of chips offers the values people actually pick: yesterday's reading, and small nudges around it. One tap sets the number without touching the stepper at all.

data-entry/quick-add

Backdating an entry

Forgot to weigh in yesterday? The date at the top of the sheet is a control. A segmented switch covers the common cases — Today and Yesterday — with Pick a date for anything older. Backdated entries drop into the right place in history; they never overwrite the goal pace silently.

Duplicate & overwrite

One reading per day keeps the trend honest. If an entry already exists for the chosen date, don't silently stack a second — ask. A small confirm dialog states the existing value and the new one, and makes Replace the deliberate choice.

Confirmation & undo

Every logged weight closes the loop with a toast: it echoes the value so the person knows exactly what landed, and carries an Undo so a fat-fingered entry is one tap from gone. The sheet closes underneath it — no extra confirmation screen.

Weight saved!

Your entry has been recorded successfully.

Don't interrupt a daily action with a modal that must be dismissed.
Accessibility. The sheet body is role="dialog" aria-modal="true" with a label; focus moves into it on open and returns to the trigger on close. Stepper buttons carry aria-labels (“Increase by 0.1 kg”) and the numeral is a live region so assistive tech announces the new value. The confirmation toast is polite, not assertive, so it never interrupts.
