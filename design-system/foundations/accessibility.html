Foundations

Accessibility

Accessibility is built into the tokens and components, not bolted on. A visible focus ring, generous touch targets, AA contrast in both themes, honoured motion preferences, and meaning that never depends on colour alone — this is the floor, met by default.

One visible focus ring

Every interactive element shares a single, high-contrast ring — a 2px --focus-ring outline with a 2px offset, shown on keyboard focus via :focus-visible. It never disappears "for looks".

a11y/focus-ring
:where(a, button, input, select, textarea, [tabindex]):focus-visible {
  outline: 2px solid var(--focus-ring);
  outline-offset: 2px;
}

Touch targets ≥ 44px

Buttons, icon buttons, switches and nav items all meet a 44 × 44px minimum hit area — comfortable for thumbs and compliant with WCAG 2.5.5. Visual size can be smaller as long as the target is padded out.

Do
The 44px target holds even when the label is short or the icon is small.
Don't
Don't ship cramped tap targets — they fail touch users and pointer-precision guidelines.

AA contrast, both themes

Text tokens are tuned to clear WCAG AA on their intended surface — 4.5:1 for body, 3:1 for large text and UI — in dark and light alike. Toggle the theme (top right) and the guarantee holds.

ForegroundOn surfaceRatio (dark)Level
--text-hi--canvas15.8 : 1AAA
--text-mid--surface-17.1 : 1AA
--text-lo--canvas4.6 : 1AA
--brand-strong--surface-19.4 : 1AAA
--text-on-gold--brand8.2 : 1AAA
--good-text--good-subtle5.9 : 1AA
The gold accent always carries dark text (--text-on-gold), never white — white on gold fails AA. This is why the primary button reads dark-on-gold in both themes.

Never colour alone

Colour-blind readers and low-contrast conditions mean hue can't be the only carrier of meaning. Every status pairs colour with an icon, sign or label.

Do
−0.4 kg +0.2 kg
Direction is shown by an arrow and a sign, so it reads without colour.
Don't
0.4 0.2
Hue alone is ambiguous — which way did the number move?

ARIA patterns

The interactive components ship with the right roles. Reuse these three whenever you compose your own.

Dialogs & sheets
<div class="au-dialog" role="dialog" aria-modal="true" aria-labelledby="t">
  <h2 id="t" class="au-dialog-title">Delete entry?</h2>
  <!-- focus is trapped; Esc closes; the scrim is inert -->
</div>
Toasts — live region
<div class="au-toast-region" role="status" aria-live="polite"> … </div>
<!-- polite announces without interrupting; the runtime creates this for you -->
The gauge — decorative arc, spoken value
a11y/gauge-label
72%
to goal
<div class="au-gauge" role="img" aria-label="72 percent to goal">
  <svg aria-hidden="true"> … </svg>
</div>

Motion preferences

Aurora honours prefers-reduced-motion: reduce globally — every duration token collapses to 1ms and the gauge / count-up scripts skip their animation, resolving straight to the final state. Motion is never required to understand or operate the UI. See Motion for the full contract.

The floor, met by default. Use the semantic components and you inherit focus, targets, contrast, motion handling and roles. When you build something new, hold it to the same six checks above.
