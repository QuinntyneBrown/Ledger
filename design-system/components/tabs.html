Tabs

An underlined strip that swaps between panels of peer content in one place — summary, trend, log — without a page change. A single gold underline marks the active tab so orientation is never in doubt.

When to use it

Reach for tabs when several views are equally important peers of one subject and only one is needed at a time. They keep a single context on screen while letting the person pivot between facets of it.

  • Use them for 3–5 peer views with short, noun-like labels.
  • Keep exactly one tab active; the gold underline is the only active indicator.
  • For switching between a handful of options that filter one value, prefer a segmented control; for top-level app destinations, use navigation.

Anatomy

Panel content for the active tab.
1 2 3 4
  • 1 Active tab — high-emphasis label.
  • 2 Inactive tab — muted until hovered.
  • 3 Indicator — the gold underline under the active tab.
  • 4 Panel — the content region below the strip.

Do & don’t

Do
Use a few short, parallel labels and keep one tab clearly active.
Don’t
Don’t cram long or uneven labels until the strip overflows and truncates.

Underlined tabs

The default. Wrap the strip and panels in [data-tabs]; aurora.js handles the swap and the active state.

<div data-tabs>
  <div class="au-tabs" role="tablist">
    <button class="au-tab is-active" data-tab="a" role="tab">Overview</button>
    <button class="au-tab" data-tab="b" role="tab">Details</button>
    <button class="au-tab" data-tab="c" role="tab">History</button>
  </div>
  <div class="au-tabpanel" data-tabpanel="a" role="tabpanel">…</div>
  <div class="au-tabpanel" data-tabpanel="b" role="tabpanel" hidden>…</div>
  <div class="au-tabpanel" data-tabpanel="c" role="tabpanel" hidden>…</div>
</div>

Segmented alternative

When the choice filters a single value rather than swapping whole panels — a chart range, say — the pill-shaped .au-segmented control reads better.

tabs/segmented
<div class="au-segmented">
  <button>Week</button>
  <button class="is-active">Month</button>
  <button>Year</button>
  <button>All</button>
</div>

Structure & classes

ClassElementRole
.au-tabsdivThe tab strip; a bottom divider runs its full width.
.au-tabbuttonOne tab. Add .is-active for the current one.
.au-tab.is-active::after—The gold underline indicator (pseudo-element).

Wiring

AttributeOnEffect
data-tabswrapperMarks a group. Must contain both the tabs and their panels.
data-tab="ID"buttonThe panel this tab reveals.
data-tabpanel="ID"panelShown when the matching tab is active; hidden otherwise.
Accessibility. Add role="tablist" to .au-tabs, role="tab" to each .au-tab, and role="tabpanel" to each panel; link them with aria-controls / aria-labelledby and mark the active tab aria-selected="true". Tabs are reachable and operable from the keyboard via the standard focus ring. Keep the wrapper and its panels together so the swap is scoped to one group.
