Spacing & layout

Every gap, pad and margin is a multiple of 4px. One rhythm makes layouts feel composed rather than arranged, keeps optical alignment honest, and means designers and engineers reach for the same twelve values.

The 4px scale

Twelve steps from a 4px hairline gap to a 96px section break. Steps compress early (fine control in dense UI) and stride later (breathing room between regions).

Reach for --space-2 / -3 inside a control, --space-4 / -6 for card padding and stacks, and --space-9 / -10 between page sections. If a value is not on the scale, it is a bug.

Containers

Three maxima cap line length and frame the two products. The app is deliberately narrow — Ledger is mobile-first — while the docs breathe wider.

Responsive breakpoints

Three ranges. The docs sidebar collapses to a drawer below tablet; the app runs mobile-width everywhere, centred in a frame on desktop.

RangeWidthBehaviour
Mobile≤ 520pxSingle column, full-bleed cards, bottom nav, sheets slide up from the base.
Tablet521 – 960pxDocs sidebar becomes an off-canvas drawer; two-column grids relax to one.
Desktop> 960pxPersistent 276px docs sidebar, on-this-page rail, full multi-column layouts.
Design mobile-first: author the narrow layout, then add breathing room and columns as space allows. The app never widens past --app-max — extra desktop space becomes frame, not stretched content.
