Aurora Foundations Motion
Foundations

Motion

Motion in Aurora is quiet and confirming, never decorative. A press dips, a card lifts, the dial sweeps once to settle. Timing comes from six duration tokens and five easings — and every one of them collapses to a hair when the reader asks for less.

Durations

Shorter for small, local changes; longer for larger travel. The named steps keep timing consistent so the whole product feels like one hand moved it.

--dur-instant80ms
--dur-fast140ms
--dur-base220ms
--dur-slow360ms
--dur-slower560ms
--dur-dial1100ms
TokenTimeFor
--dur-instant80msColour & opacity flips — hover tints, focus rings.
--dur-fast140msButton presses, small state toggles, chip selection.
--dur-base220msThe default: switches, tab underlines, scrims.
--dur-slow360msDialogs and toasts entering, card lifts.
--dur-slower560msSheets sliding up, the chart drawing in.
--dur-dial1100msThe signature gauge sweep — one deliberate arrival.

Easing

The curve is the character. Standard handles most UI; emphasized gives reveals a springy, best-in-class settle; the directional pair covers enters and exits.

Standard
cubic-bezier(.2, 0, 0, 1)

The default. Quick out, gentle in. Most transitions.

Emphasized
cubic-bezier(.16, 1, .3, 1)

Springy settle for reveals — the dial, sheets, pops.

Out
cubic-bezier(0, 0, .2, 1)

Decelerate. Elements entering the screen.

In
cubic-bezier(.4, 0, 1, 1)

Accelerate. Elements leaving the screen.

In-out
cubic-bezier(.4, 0, .2, 1)

Symmetric. Movement that starts and ends on screen.

Presses use transform: translateY + scale at --dur-fast; the card lift rides --dur-base on --ease-emphasized; the gauge sweeps once over --dur-dial and the number counts up in step.

The reduced-motion contract

Motion is an enhancement, never a requirement. When the reader has prefers-reduced-motion: reduce set, Aurora redefines every duration token to 1ms — animations resolve instantly to their final state, and nothing is lost.

@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce) {
  :root {
    --dur-instant: 1ms; --dur-fast: 1ms; --dur-base: 1ms;
    --dur-slow: 1ms; --dur-slower: 1ms; --dur-dial: 1ms;
  }
}
Because components read the tokens rather than hardcoding times, this single block disables motion everywhere at once. The runtime also skips the gauge sweep and count-up JS when reduced motion is requested. The ?static=1 flag on any page freezes animation the same way for screenshots.
